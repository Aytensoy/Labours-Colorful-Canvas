A Crossroads of Faith and History

Welcome to İznik, a town not just famous for its stunning tiles, but a place that stands as a monumental crossroads of history. The scenes you are about to color witnessed events that shaped the very foundations of Christianity for billions around the world. Before it was İznik, it was the great Roman city of Nicaea.

The First Council of Nicaea (325 AD)

In the year 325 AD, Emperor Constantine the Great, facing deep disagreements within the rapidly spreading Christian faith, summoned bishops from across the known world to this very city. This historic gathering, the first-ever ecumenical (worldwide) council, was tasked with unifying the church by defining its essential beliefs.

The Lasting Legacy: The Nicene Creed

The most significant outcome of this council was the creation of the Nicene Creed. This powerful statement of faith articulated the core beliefs held by the vast majority of Christians, providing a unifying text that could be recited by believers everywhere. Today, nearly 1,700 years later, the Nicene Creed remains a central and revered prayer in Catholic, Orthodox, and many Protestant churches worldwide. It is a living link to the historical events that took place right here.

As you bring these pages to life, you are connecting with a legacy of faith and history that began in ancient Nicaea and continues to echo through the centuries.
